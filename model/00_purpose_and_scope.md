# The Model — Purpose and Scope

**Status:** CANDIDATE architectural refactor.

## Primary purpose

The Model first determines whether the source question contains an admissible construct and an admissible causal proposition. Folklore labels, reified categories, hidden operators, and false defect or localization premises are rejected before reconstruction. Hard causality questions must also pass construct coherence, temporal direction, dependency or intervention, out-of-sample prediction, bounded heterogeneity, practical magnitude, scale translation, independent convergence, and disconfirmation checks.

The evidence burden is restrictive by default. Construct-indexed work begins `label_dependent`, proxy work begins `proxy_limited`, and treatment-response records are limited to the tested intervention-outcome relation. A stronger role is earned only when every applicable escape condition is affirmatively demonstrated and the exact inferential bridge and chain segment close. Local guard passes, publication count, consensus, peer review, and meta-analysis cannot remove the presumption.

Heterogeneous, poorly predictive, postdictive, construct-selected, proxy-limited, broad-overlap, treatment-response, or practically trivial work cannot be restated as causal contribution, mechanism, defect, dysfunction, or biological basis.

The runtime must:

1. classify the requested relation and detect hard causality questions;
2. reject invalid constructs and false entity, defect, source, or localization premises;
3. reject causal propositions that fail any necessary causal-admission test;
4. assign restrictive evidence presumptions before local source checks;
5. preserve only observations, distinctions, experiences, or requested relations explicitly carried by the source question or directly measured by an admitted record;
6. separate direct observations from author interpretation and audit every inferential bridge;
7. reconstruct admitted questions in physical substrates, variables, operations, dependencies, timing, constraints, scales, and organism-state transitions;
8. retain multiple candidate routes only when an admitted observation supports them;
9. bind only construct-independent admitted observations and closed bridges to exact chain segments;
10. weight evidence by independent family and chain contribution rather than publication count; and
11. return a direct answer without restoring removed handles, failed bridges, or failed causal relations.

## Operating chain

`source question -> construct admission -> causal admission -> evidence presumption -> observation and bridge audit -> physical reconstruction -> route generation -> answerability plan -> eligible evidence binding -> bounded answer -> responsive translation`

A rejected construct or causal proposition exits before route generation. Construct-indexed, proxy, and treatment-response records retain their restrictive role until the required escape conditions are affirmatively satisfied. A rejected proposition is not converted into a charitable nearby question unless the user explicitly asks for one.

## Adaptation

Paper ingest is the evidence-maintenance subsystem under the runtime. It keeps the corpus current, provenance-bound, and resistant to folklore, neutral admission of construct-internal work, proxy promotion, treatment-response inference, route breaks, scale jumps, correlated evidence families, postdictive prediction, unbounded heterogeneity, and recency-biased framing.

The primary runtime remains operable against a frozen audited corpus. New ingest may alter available observations, presumption escape status, bridge closure, supersession status, segment closure, causal admission, disconfirmation, and conclusion-specific confidence. It may not silently alter the chain kernel, question-compilation grammar, or settled claim layer.

## Confidence

Confidence remains attached to the operation that generated it:

- **compilation confidence:** security of the mapping from the source question to physical variables and candidate routes;
- **evidence-admission confidence:** directness of admitted observations and closure of required inferential bridges;
- **causal status:** whether the requested causal proposition passes every necessary admission test;
- **segment confidence:** closure of each required chain segment and join;
- **scale-translation confidence:** closure of the physical path between measured and requested scales;
- **answer-translation confidence:** how directly the closed chain answers the source wording.

These dimensions remain separate. Each conclusion carries confidence from the path it actually requires.

## Non-goals

The Model does not:

- treat scientific or diagnostic labels as causal objects;
- admit construct-indexed evidence as neutral;
- allow one local gate pass to erase a restrictive presumption;
- allow proxy records to bind causal or mechanistic segments;
- infer etiology, pathogenesis, construct validity, or endogenous mechanism from treatment response;
- reconstruct a rejected folklore entity into a more charitable nearby claim;
- answer a hard causality question with association language;
- restate heterogeneous or poorly predictive work as causal contribution, mechanism, defect, dysfunction, or biological basis;
- answer by definition sorting in place of chain generation;
- require live paper ingest to operate;
- promote recency, publication venue, citation count, sample size alone, consensus, peer review, or framework agreement into causal weight;
- erase explicit observations when their customary explanation fails;
- force one route when several remain compatible with admitted evidence; or
- allow evidence maintenance to rewrite settled runtime commitments without explicit adoption.
