# The Model — Purpose and Scope

**Status:** CANDIDATE architectural refactor.

## Primary purpose

The Model first determines whether the source question contains an admissible construct and an admissible causal proposition. Folklore labels, reified categories, hidden operators, and false defect or localization premises are rejected before reconstruction. Hard causality questions must also pass construct coherence, temporal direction, dependency or intervention, out-of-sample prediction, bounded heterogeneity, practical magnitude, scale translation, independent convergence, and disconfirmation checks.

Heterogeneous, poorly predictive, postdictive, label-selected, proxy-limited, broad-overlap, or practically trivial work cannot be restated as causal contribution, mechanism, defect, dysfunction, or biological basis.

The runtime must:

1. classify the requested relation and detect hard causality questions;
2. reject invalid constructs and false entity, defect, source, or localization premises;
3. reject causal propositions that fail any necessary causal-admission test;
4. preserve only observations, distinctions, experiences, or requested relations explicitly carried by the source question;
5. reconstruct admitted questions in physical substrates, variables, operations, dependencies, timing, constraints, scales, and organism-state transitions;
6. retain multiple candidate routes only when an admitted observation supports them;
7. identify the measurements, predictions, and discriminating observations required to close those routes;
8. bind reviewed evidence to the exact chain segments, joins, predictions, and premises it supports or breaks;
9. weight evidence by independent family and chain contribution rather than publication count; and
10. return a direct answer without restoring removed handles or failed causal relations.

## Operating chain

`source question -> construct admission -> causal admission -> observation preservation -> physical reconstruction -> route generation -> answerability plan -> evidence-family binding -> bounded answer -> responsive translation`

A rejected construct or causal proposition exits before route generation. It is not converted into a charitable nearby question unless the user explicitly asks for one.

## Adaptation

Paper ingest is the evidence-maintenance subsystem under the runtime. It keeps the corpus current, provenance-bound, and resistant to folklore, proxy promotion, route breaks, scale jumps, correlated evidence families, postdictive prediction, unbounded heterogeneity, and recency-biased framing.

The primary runtime remains operable against a frozen reviewed corpus. New ingest may alter available evidence, supersession status, segment closure, causal admission, disconfirmation, and conclusion-specific confidence. It may not silently alter the chain kernel, question-compilation grammar, or settled claim layer.

## Confidence

Confidence remains attached to the operation that generated it:

- **compilation confidence:** security of the mapping from the source question to physical variables and candidate routes;
- **causal status:** whether the requested causal proposition passes every necessary admission test;
- **segment confidence:** closure of each required chain segment and join;
- **scale-translation confidence:** closure of the physical path between measured and requested scales;
- **answer-translation confidence:** how directly the closed chain answers the source wording.

These dimensions remain separate. Each conclusion carries confidence from the path it actually requires.

## Non-goals

The Model does not:

- treat scientific or diagnostic labels as causal objects;
- reconstruct a rejected folklore entity into a more charitable nearby claim;
- answer a hard causality question with association language;
- restate heterogeneous or poorly predictive work as causal contribution, mechanism, defect, dysfunction, or biological basis;
- answer by definition sorting in place of chain generation;
- require live paper ingest to operate;
- promote recency, publication venue, citation count, sample size alone, or framework agreement into causal weight;
- erase explicit observations when their customary explanation fails;
- force one route when several remain compatible with admitted evidence; or
- allow evidence maintenance to rewrite settled runtime commitments without explicit adoption.
