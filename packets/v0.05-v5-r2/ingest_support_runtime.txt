# The Model — Purpose and Scope

**Status:** ADOPTED — v0.05 v5, July 11, 2026.

The structural and deterministic validation suite passed at adoption. Live-provider conformance remains an explicit validation debt and is not represented as completed.

## Primary purpose

The Model determines what target identity, construct, relation, and causal burden a question actually contains. It keeps speaker intent separate from corpus operations, admits evidence from measured data rather than published narrative, and reconstructs only the physical relations that survive identity, construct, design, counterfactual, bridge, and chain checks.

The shared rules are domain-neutral:

1. generate target identity from recoverable operations rather than inherit it from terminology;
2. keep speaker intent separate from corpus-defined and measured targets;
3. keep every explanation on one continuous physical surface;
4. bind biological explanation through metabolically maintained state;
5. reject reified labels and hidden operators that supply causal content by name;
6. classify the exact target relation before assigning causal status;
7. extract the actual observations, comparator, effect distribution, heterogeneity, attrition, durability, measurement validity, and analysis limits;
8. treat every increase from observation to shared target, construct, direction, mechanism, effectiveness, named process, or cross-scale meaning as a separate inferential bridge;
9. begin observational evidence at descriptive or more restrictive scope;
10. allow intervention evidence to earn causality for the exact manipulated relation when its full data and counterfactual burden closes;
11. require separate bridges for target identity, transport, etiology, endogenous mechanism, construct validity, and cross-scale explanation;
12. bind evidence by independent family and exact chain contribution rather than paper count; and
13. return the strongest data-supported answer without restoring failed narrative claims.

Construct dependence and measurement distance remain independent restrictions. Construct-indexed evidence begins `label_dependent`. Proxy or surrogate evidence begins `proxy_limited`. Study design cannot erase those restrictions by itself.

## Target identity

Names may compress generated identities. They may not generate identities by compression.

Before causal weighting, the runtime removes each target name and asks what independently measurable or reproducibly constructed relation remains. It then asks what generator makes the surviving observations instances of the same target. A generator may be a stable physical interaction structure, equation, model-defined state, algorithmic reconstruction, measurement standard, demonstrated organism-state relation, or explicit conditional construction.

Identity shortcuts are allowed when they point to a recoverable generator and remain within its model, scale, regime, apparatus, task, population, construction, uncertainty, and transport limits. Repeated terminology, validated instruments, disciplinary familiarity, citation volume, randomization, and intervention response do not create target identity.

The runtime builds two records:

- the **speaker-intent record**, which identifies the observable relation, practical scope, and distinction the asker cares about; and
- the **corpus-identity record**, which identifies how sources select, measure, reconstruct, aggregate, threshold, and transport the target.

The asker controls the intended question. The asker does not supply construct unity or causal closure. The corpus supplies operations and observations. Corpus agreement does not establish identity without a recoverable generator.

When multiple non-equivalent operationalizations share a name, the name remains a grouping handle and each relation is evaluated separately. Heterogeneity preserves a common identity only when exposed variables and routes generate and predict the observed variation. Terms such as complexity, multidimensionality, heterogeneous presentation, individual variation, context dependence, and task impurity do not protect an ungenerated identity.

## Universal physical floor

Every admitted cause, operation, state, constraint, and transition is physical. Chemistry is organized physical interaction. Biology is chemically and energetically maintained physical organization. Behavior is organism-state change produced through that maintained organization.

The forces and constraints acting in organisms belong to the same physical world that forms stars, drives fusion, moves matter, and constrains every other system. Scale changes organization, reachable states, and useful description. It does not add a new causal category.

An answer may stop at the shortest sufficient scale for the question. Every stated segment must remain physically realizable, decomposable downward, and compatible upward through the chain.

## Biological metabolic binding

At biological scope, metabolically maintained state is the obligatory binding layer between physical or chemical change and biological operation. Metabolics drives biology because biological organization persists and changes only through ongoing energy and material throughput under physical constraint.

A biological or behavioral explanation therefore identifies, at the resolution supported by the evidence:

1. the physical perturbation or constraint;
2. the chemical, electrical, mechanical, thermal, energetic, or material carrier;
3. the change in metabolically maintained state;
4. the biological operation that changes availability, weighting, stabilization, blocking, release, maintenance, repair, or transition;
5. the resulting organism-state transition or behavior.

Missing segments remain explicit. “Metabolism,” “energy,” “activation,” “signaling,” “regulation,” “information,” and “network change” cannot substitute for a physical variable, carrier, state change, and downstream dependency.

## Operating chain

`source question -> speaker-intent reconstruction -> corpus target-identity reconstruction -> identity disposition -> construct admission -> exact target-relation classification -> evidence design and data extraction -> inferential-bridge audit -> causal admission by target -> physical reconstruction -> metabolic binding at biological scope -> route discrimination -> eligible evidence binding -> bounded answer`

Evidence retrieval may begin to resolve target identity, but causal weighting waits until exact target relations and identity dispositions are emitted.

A failed broad claim does not erase a valid narrower observation, constructed measure, or intervention relation. A valid intervention relation does not automatically close target identity, transport, the untreated system's origin, endogenous route, or full mechanism.

A decisive intervention may establish the exact manipulation-to-outcome relation while the internal metabolic route remains partial. The answer must preserve that causal result and expose the open route rather than either rejecting the effect or inventing a mechanism.

## Generic and domain-specific layers

The kernel and primary runtime contain only general mechanics. Domain cartridges supply specialized examples, known inference failures, translation burdens, and regression cases. They do not provide target-identity trigger lists.

The neuroscience cartridge owns examples involving diagnostic categories, named brain regions, psychiatric treatment claims, rating scales, imaging, biomarkers, stimulation, and named processes such as “neuroplasticity.” It also applies the physical-continuity rule to ionic gradients, electrical fields, transmitter release, intracellular chemistry, glial and vascular support, oxygen and substrate delivery, tissue maintenance, and organism-state transitions.

## Evidence admission

### Observational evidence

Observational evidence may fully support a descriptive relation. Causal promotion requires affirmative closure of the burden appropriate to the claimed target, including target identity, temporal direction, counterfactual adequacy, alternative-route testing, practical magnitude, stable conditions, independence, and every claimed mechanism or scale bridge. Adjustment, recurrence, large samples, or causal vocabulary cannot substitute for those dependencies.

### Intervention evidence

Intervention evidence is evaluated from manipulation integrity, comparator, timing, measurement validity, full response distribution, practical magnitude, heterogeneity, specificity, durability, attrition, adverse outcomes, replication, and disconfirmation.

A decisive controlled intervention may establish intervention-to-outcome causality. Treatment or intervention status neither grants nor denies that result. Randomization binds to the object actually assigned and the outcome actually recorded. Broader claims about target identity, transport, etiology, endogenous mechanism, construct validity, or cross-scale explanation remain separate targets.

### Narrative discipline

The actual data outrank the abstract, discussion, conclusion, guideline, review, or consensus narrative. Terms such as “effective,” “treats,” “normalizes,” “induces,” or “mechanistic” may appear only when the data support their exact target, comparator, practical, temporal, identity, and physical scope.

## Confidence

Confidence remains attached to the operation that generated it:

- **identity confidence:** security of the generator, operational equivalence, and transport of the named target;
- **compilation confidence:** security of the mapping from speaker intent and source operations to exact target relations and physical variables;
- **evidence-admission confidence:** completeness and validity of the extracted data and inferential bridges;
- **causal-target status:** whether the exact target relation passes its design-specific causal burden;
- **segment confidence:** closure of each required physical, chemical, metabolic, biological, and behavioral segment and join;
- **scale-translation confidence:** closure of the physical path between measured and requested scales;
- **answer-translation confidence:** how directly the closed relation answers the source wording.

## Non-goals

The Model does not:

- require every accepted scientific shortcut to be redefined inline when a recoverable generator exists;
- allow a name, score, threshold, classifier, or repeated convention to create target identity;
- use unexplained heterogeneity to preserve a unitary target;
- introduce a separate causal currency for biology, neural tissue, or behavior;
- allow effects to jump between named components without a carrier and state transition;
- use metabolism or energy as a replacement magic noun;
- treat labels, regions, faculties, therapies, or named processes as causal objects by name;
- treat observational recurrence as causal closure;
- reject a decisive intervention merely because ordinary evidence in the field is weak;
- infer target identity, transport, etiology, or endogenous mechanism from intervention response alone;
- translate statistical significance into effectiveness;
- hide response heterogeneity behind a group mean;
- rename a proxy or pre-post measurement as a biological process;
- allow publication volume, prestige, peer review, consensus, or meta-analysis to repair invalid inputs;
- erase valid narrow observations when broader claims fail; or
- allow domain-specific assumptions to leak silently into the generic kernel.

target_identity:
  status: candidate
  layer: shared_kernel
  purpose: >
    Require every named target to expose a recoverable identity generator before evidence can bind to the name.
    Names may compress generated identities. They may not generate identities by compression.

  applies_before:
    - evidence_retrieval_for_causal_weight
    - design_admission
    - causal_weighting
    - mechanism_search
    - effectiveness_language
    - cross_scale_translation

  governing_question: >
    What independently measurable relation remains after the target name is removed, and what generator makes
    the surviving observations instances of the same target rather than merely members of one verbal category?

  identity_sources:
    speaker_intent:
      role: identify_the_relation_scope_and_distinction_the_asker_cares_about
      cannot_supply:
        - construct_unity
        - causal_closure
        - physical_identity
        - corpus_wide_operational_equivalence
    corpus_operations:
      role: expose_how_sources_select_construct_measure_aggregate_and_transport_the_target
      cannot_supply_by_agreement_alone:
        - identity_from_repeated_terminology
        - unity_from_shared_scale_or_threshold
        - validity_from_citation_volume
        - causal_identity_from_intervention_response

  identity_dispositions:
    generated_identity_closed:
      construct_disposition: admitted
      rule: >
        A recoverable generator, variables, realization or reconstruction procedure, conditions, uncertainty,
        and reproduction path establish the target at the claimed scope.
    generated_identity_conditional:
      construct_disposition: admitted
      rule: >
        The identity is generated only within an explicit model, scale, regime, apparatus, task, population,
        or construction. Evidence binds only within those stated conditions.
    constructed_measurement_identity:
      construct_disposition: admitted
      rule: >
        An algorithm, score, threshold, classifier, or aggregation rule reproducibly creates the target.
        Evidence binds to that construction and does not automatically bind to a broader natural kind.
    grouping_handle_only:
      construct_disposition: grouping_handle_only
      rule: >
        Multiple non-equivalent relations are grouped without a demonstrated common generator. Preserve the
        name only as a conversational, administrative, historical, or indexing handle.
    identity_unresolved:
      construct_disposition: underspecified
      rule: >
        The available speaker and corpus records do not establish what the target denotes or whether the
        operationalizations share identity.
    premise_rejected:
      construct_disposition: premise_rejected
      rule: >
        The name supplies the entire object and no independent observation or construction survives removal.

  recoverable_generator_requirements:
    fields:
      - generator_type
      - generator_definition_or_pointer
      - variables_or_inputs
      - realization_reconstruction_or_measurement_procedure
      - aggregation_threshold_or_classification_rule
      - boundary_conditions_and_regime
      - uncertainty_and_error_model
      - reproducibility_or_recovery_path
      - sensitivity_to_plausible_alternate_constructions
      - transport_scope
    generator_types:
      - physical_invariant_or_stable_interaction_structure
      - equation_or_model_defined_state
      - algorithmic_reconstruction
      - measurement_or_realization_standard
      - demonstrated_organism_state_relation
      - explicit_conditional_construction
    shortcut_rule: >
      A source may use an identity shortcut without restating the complete generator when the pointer is
      recoverable and the current use remains within the generator's demonstrated conditions.

  speaker_interrogation:
    objective: resolve_requested_relation_without_asking_the_speaker_to_prove_construct_validity
    required_record:
      - source_wording
      - intended_referent_candidates
      - requested_scope
      - requested_time_horizon
      - distinctions_that_change_the_answer
      - selected_or_inferred_referent
      - unresolved_material_ambiguity
    interaction_rule: >
      Reconstruct silently when all plausible interpretations produce the same bounded answer. When different
      interpretations produce materially different answers, present the smallest useful fork using observable
      relations rather than asking for another verbal definition.
    control_rule: >
      The asker controls the intended question and practical scope. The asker does not supply target unity,
      physical identity, operational equivalence, or causal closure.

  corpus_interrogation:
    per_source_required_record:
      - source_label
      - sample_selection_and_grouping
      - exact_observations
      - measurement_operations
      - task_apparatus_and_environmental_conditions
      - timing_and_state_conditions
      - aggregation_threshold_or_classification_rule
      - discarded_or_unobserved_cases
      - inferred_target_after_measurement
      - claimed_transport_scope
      - imported_identity_pointer
    cross_source_tests:
      - same_label_same_operationalization
      - same_label_different_operationalization
      - different_label_same_operationalization
      - cross_measure_prediction
      - intervention_transfer_across_operationalizations
      - sign_magnitude_and_distribution_stability
      - measurement_invariance
      - alternate_construction_sensitivity
      - condition_and_population_transport
      - identity_breaking_counterexamples
    search_trees:
      definition_tree:
        - original_and_revised_definitions
        - competing_definitions
        - standards_and_instrument_manuals
        - inclusion_exclusion_and_threshold_changes
      operationalization_tree:
        - tasks
        - scales_and_questionnaires
        - behavioral_events
        - assays_and_biomarkers
        - coding_and_reconstruction_procedures
        - latent_variable_and_aggregation_models
      failure_tree:
        - measurement_noninvariance
        - low_cross_task_or_cross_instrument_relation
        - subgroup_instability
        - sign_reversal
        - poor_out_of_sample_prediction
        - intervention_nontransfer
        - inconsistent_factor_or_component_structure
        - same_label_divergent_outputs
        - different_labels_same_outputs

  heterogeneity_rule: >
    Heterogeneity preserves a common identity only when an exposed generator accounts for the variation and
    predicts how conditions produce the different observations. Complexity, multidimensionality, heterogeneous
    presentation, task impurity, individual variation, and context dependence are unresolved demands unless
    their differentiating variables and routes are generated.

  relation_separation:
    required_relations:
      - speaker_intended_target
      - source_defined_target
      - exact_measured_relation
      - constructed_or_aggregated_measure
      - claimed_broader_target
      - claimed_transport_relation
    evidence_binding_rule: >
      Evidence binds first to the exact measured or constructed relation. It may bind to a shared named target
      only after operational equivalence, generator identity, and transport close at the claimed scope.
    randomization_rule: >
      Randomization applies to the object actually assigned and the outcome actually recorded. It cannot create
      target identity, isolate an unmeasured active component, or validate a broader construct by response.
    magnitude_rule: Effect magnitude cannot repair target identity, operational equivalence, or transport.

  failure_conditions:
    - name_supplies_target_identity
    - definition_is_only_a_synonym_or_adjacent_label
    - non_equivalent_relations_are_compressed_as_one_target
    - score_threshold_or_classifier_is_treated_as_direct_access_to_a_broader_entity
    - condition_task_or_measurement_operations_disappear_in_translation
    - protocol_bound_change_is_renamed_as_general_trait_or_real_world_change
    - heterogeneity_is_used_to_protect_identity_without_a_generator
    - repeated_corpus_agreement_is_used_as_identity_evidence
    - intervention_response_is_used_as_construct_validation

  preservation_rule: >
    Identity failure does not erase observations. Preserve every exact measured event, distribution, transition,
    construction, and protocol-bound relation at its own scope, then route broader failed claims to the appropriate
    label-dependent, constructed-measurement, descriptive, proxy, disconfirmation, method, or provenance channel.

  invariants:
    target_identity_is_generated_not_inherited: true
    names_may_compress_but_may_not_create_identity: true
    speaker_intent_and_corpus_identity_remain_separate: true
    evidence_binds_to_exact_relation_before_shared_name: true
    heterogeneity_must_be_generated: true
    design_strength_does_not_repair_identity: true
    identity_shortcuts_require_recoverable_generators: true

evidence_admission:
  status: candidate
  layer: shared_kernel
  purpose: >
    Admit evidence from the measured data rather than the source narrative. Observational, interventional,
    synthetic, and review records receive only the causal scope earned by their design, measurements,
    effect distribution, inferential bridges, and exposed dependency path.

  default_posture:
    source_claim_status: unadmitted_assertion
    causal_weight: zero
    mechanism_weight: zero
    burden_holder: claimant
    absence_of_disconfirmation_is_support: false
    author_interpretation_is_evidence: false
    peer_review_is_evidence: false
    consensus_is_evidence: false
    meta_analysis_repairs_invalid_inputs: false
    local_guard_passes_create_causal_eligibility: false

  evidence_axes:
    design_mode:
      - observational
      - longitudinal_observational
      - natural_experiment
      - quasi_experimental
      - interventional
      - simulation_or_model
      - synthesis
    construct_relation:
      - construct_independent
      - construct_indexed
      - construct_internal
    measurement_relation:
      - direct_target_measurement
      - direct_dependency_measurement
      - proxy
      - surrogate
      - composite_or_scale
      - self_report
      - model_derived
    target_relation:
      - descriptive_distribution
      - predictive_relation
      - intervention_to_outcome
      - endogenous_dependency
      - etiology_or_origin
      - mechanism_or_route
      - cross_scale_translation
    chain_exposure:
      - observation_only
      - dependency_partially_exposed
      - dependency_directly_exposed

  data_first_admission:
    rule: >
      The admissible claim is generated from the actual observations and design. The abstract, conclusion,
      intervention label, diagnostic label, theoretical vocabulary, and author interpretation cannot increase
      causal scope beyond the data.
    required_data_fields:
      - exact_outcome_definition
      - exact_exposure_or_intervention
      - baseline_distribution
      - comparator_or_counterfactual
      - sample_and_exclusion_flow
      - effect_distribution_not_only_group_mean
      - effect_magnitude
      - uncertainty
      - overlap_and_response_heterogeneity
      - missingness_attrition_and_crossover
      - timing_and_durability
      - dose_or_exposure_relation_when_applicable
      - adverse_or_opposing_outcomes
      - measurement_validity_and_noise
      - preregistration_outcome_switching_and_analysis_multiplicity
      - replication_and_condition_stability
    narrative_conflict_rule: >
      When the narrative is stronger than the measured effect, the data control and the stronger narrative is
      recorded as a stripped assertion. Statistical significance, a named therapy, or a named biological process
      never supplies practical magnitude, consistency, specificity, durability, or mechanism.
    claim_language_requirements:
      effective: Requires a stated target, comparator, practical threshold, response distribution, durability, and adverse-outcome scope.
      induces: Requires temporal precedence, direct measurement of the induced change, comparator control, persistence, and specificity.
      mechanism: Requires the dependency path that carries the intervention or exposure into the measured outcome.
      biological_change: Requires a directly interpretable substrate and variable rather than a proxy label or generic process noun.

  presumptive_classification:
    rule: >
      Classification occurs before local gates. The starting state is determined by construct dependence,
      measurement distance, design mode, and target relation. Stronger binding is affirmatively earned for the
      exact relation rather than granted to a paper or evidence category as a whole.
    classes:
      construct_indexed:
        triggers:
          - samples selected by diagnosis or theoretical category
          - outcomes defined by the same instrument threshold or construct
          - tasks or assays selected because they are conventionally associated with the construct
          - synthesis whose inputs share the construct assumption
        presumed_closure_state: label_dependent
        presumed_support_role: label_dependent_observation
        escape_requires:
          - target relation specified independently of the construct label
          - operationalization independent of the construct-defining instrument or threshold
          - stability under relabeling threshold perturbation and alternative operationalization
          - replication outside construct-selected sampling when the target claim exceeds that sampling frame
          - direct prediction or dependency evidence independent of group membership
        count_can_escape: false
        meta_analysis_can_escape: false
      proxy_or_surrogate:
        triggers:
          - biomarker
          - imaging signal
          - assay output
          - classifier score
          - surrogate endpoint
          - downstream readout
          - model-derived latent score
        presumed_closure_state: proxy_limited
        presumed_support_role: proxy_only
        rule: >
          The record binds only the measured proxy relation until a physical translation path closes. If the
          measurement directly exposes the required dependency, classify that relation as direct dependency
          evidence rather than upgrading the proxy by language or recurrence.
        count_can_escape: false
      observational_record:
        triggers:
          - no controlled manipulation of the proposed source variable
          - counterfactual supplied only by adjustment matching modeling or group comparison
        presumed_closure_state: descriptive_only
        presumed_support_role: descriptive_support
        causal_upgrade_requires:
          - construct-independent target and measurement
          - temporal order appropriate to the claim
          - a relation strong and specific enough to discriminate the proposed dependency
          - stable direction and magnitude across relevant conditions
          - realistic alternative routes and confounding paths tested rather than narrated away
          - negative controls or equivalent failure probes
          - practical magnitude relative to variance overlap and noise
          - independent replication using distinct operationalizations
          - every required scale and mechanism bridge closed for the target causal claim
        rule: >
          Observational evidence is not automatically causal. Exceptionally strong observations may earn causal
          weight only when they affirmatively close the required dependency and alternative-route burden. Merely
          recurring, adjusting covariates, or using causal vocabulary does not supply that closure.
      intervention_record:
        triggers:
          - deliberate manipulation of a specified variable or route
          - controlled exposure intended to change a measured outcome
        presumed_closure_state: unresolved
        presumed_support_role: provenance_only
        eligible_target: intervention_to_outcome
        admission_requires:
          - manipulation_integrity
          - valid comparator_or_counterfactual
          - temporal_precedence
          - measurement_validity
          - effect_magnitude_and_full_response_distribution
          - bounded_or_generated_heterogeneity
          - specificity_against_relevant_alternative_routes
          - durability_appropriate_to_the_claim
          - attrition_missingness_and_crossover_bounded
          - replication_or_internal_strength_proportionate_to_claim_scope
          - no_stronger_disconfirmation
        rule: >
          An intervention can establish that the manipulated variable caused the measured outcome under the tested
          conditions when the data close these requirements. High consistency, large magnitude, specificity, and
          replication can make intervention evidence load-bearing. Treatment or intervention status neither grants
          nor denies causality by itself.
        scope_rule: >
          Causal admission attaches to the exact manipulated relation. Etiology, endogenous mechanism, construct
          validity, and cross-scale explanation require their own bridges. A treatment target does not identify the
          untreated system's origin unless those additional dependencies close.
      direct_construct_independent:
        triggers:
          - variables specified independently of diagnosis theory or proxy status
          - sampling and measurement do not require the target construct
          - the required dependency is directly measured or manipulated
        presumed_closure_state: unresolved
        presumed_support_role: provenance_only
        rule: >
          Construct independence removes label dependence but grants no causal support by itself. The exact bridge,
          segment, target relation, and applicable data requirements must still close.
    upgrade_rule: >
      A stronger closure state is relation-specific. One local pass cannot upgrade the whole record. Missing data,
      untested alternatives, or incomplete bridges preserve the restrictive state for the affected claim.

  claim_decomposition:
    required_parts:
      - direct_observation
      - sampling_and_grouping_operation
      - measurement_operation
      - analytical_transformation
      - intervention_or_exposure_operation
      - construct_assignment
      - direction_inference
      - cross_scale_inference
      - causal_or_mechanistic_conclusion
    rule: >
      Preserve direct observations and explicit operations. Treat every transition from observation to construct,
      individual state, direction, scale, mechanism, defect, dysfunction, treatment efficacy, or biological meaning
      as a separate inferential bridge.

  bridge_audit:
    required_fields:
      - bridge_id
      - source_statement
      - source_observation
      - target_claim
      - bridge_type
      - required_dependencies
      - direct_support
      - contrary_evidence
      - hidden_assumptions
      - shared_assumption_family
      - status
    bridge_types:
      - observation_to_construct
      - group_difference_to_individual_state
      - correlation_to_direction
      - location_to_source
      - perturbability_to_control
      - proxy_to_mechanism
      - intervention_to_measured_outcome
      - intervention_effect_to_treatment_effectiveness
      - intervention_target_to_endogenous_route
      - treatment_effect_to_etiology
      - measured_change_to_named_process
      - source_scale_to_target_scale
      - retrospective_to_predictive
      - prediction_to_causation
      - statistical_to_practical_significance
      - heterogeneity_to_unitary_route
    admission_rule: >
      A bridge closes only when the required dependency is supported by the actual data under the conditions needed
      by the target claim. Familiar terminology, author confidence, citation volume, recurrence, or field convention
      cannot close it.

  hostile_reading_protocol:
    required_questions:
      - What was directly observed before interpretation?
      - What are the full outcome and response distributions rather than the headline mean?
      - What comparator or counterfactual was actually observed?
      - Which effects are practically large stable durable and specific?
      - Which findings are driven by attrition missingness thresholding task choice or analysis flexibility?
      - Which claim components were introduced by labels instruments models or author language?
      - Which causal direction was measured and which was inferred?
      - Which scale crossings lack an explicit physical carrier?
      - Does an intervention close only the manipulated relation or also the claimed endogenous route?
      - Which nulls reversals overlaps and failed predictions are compressed out of the narrative?
      - Which papers repeat the same assumption family rather than provide independent closure?
    rule: >
      Read to locate inferential debt. Retain the strongest claim generated by the data after unsupported bridges
      are removed, even when that claim is much narrower than the published narrative.

  corpus_level_rules:
    - The corpus is a collection of measurements and assertions, not a trusted body of conclusions.
    - Purely observational evidence begins descriptive or more restricted and earns causal scope only by closing the full burden for the target relation.
    - Intervention evidence is judged by manipulation integrity and the actual effect distribution, not by the word treatment.
    - A decisive intervention may establish intervention-to-outcome causality even when ordinary treatment evidence does not.
    - Intervention-to-outcome causality does not automatically establish etiology, endogenous mechanism, or construct validity.
    - Construct-indexed literature remains label-dependent until it affirmatively escapes the construct.
    - Proxy literature remains proxy-limited until the required physical translation closes.
    - Repetition of one inferential bridge across papers counts as one shared assumption family, not convergence.
    - A synthesis inherits construct, measurement, selection, design, and bridge failures from its inputs.
    - Heterogeneous effects cannot be averaged into a unitary mechanism when route-generating conditions remain unknown.
    - Predictive weakness is positive evidence against a proposed unitary causal relation when that relation should predict the target.
    - Broad overlap is positive evidence against defect, dysfunction, or individual-state assignment from group means.
    - The model must prefer a sparse data-supported result over a rich unsupported causal narrative.

  output_requirements:
    - evidence_axes
    - presumptive_class
    - presumed_closure_state
    - data_fields_available
    - data_fields_missing
    - admitted_observations
    - stripped_assertions
    - bridge_ledger
    - shared_assumption_families
    - strongest_surviving_claim
    - eligible_causal_target
    - causal_weight
    - causal_rejection_basis

  forbidden_shortcuts:
    - narrative_strength_as_data_strength
    - intervention_label_as_causal_admission
    - treatment_name_as_effectiveness
    - statistically_significant_as_effective
    - pre_post_change_without_counterfactual_as_treatment_effect
    - measured_proxy_change_as_named_biological_process
    - observational_recurrence_as_causal_closure
    - local_guard_pass_as_global_upgrade
    - treatment_target_as_endogenous_mechanism
    - publication_as_admission
    - author_conclusion_as_binding
    - peer_review_as_validation
    - consensus_as_causal_weight
    - meta_analysis_as_construct_repair
    - repeated_inference_as_independent_convergence
    - hedged_causal_language_as_descriptive_neutrality

===== SOURCE: ingest/purpose.md =====

# Ingest Support Runtime

**Status:** CANDIDATE.

## Role

Ingest maintains the evidence substrate used by the question runtime. It extracts the actual data, classifies evidence design and target relation, separates observations from narrative, audits inferential bridges, and assigns relation-specific support roles. It does not define the user’s question or transfer a source conclusion into the final answer.

## Required boundary

Ingest may update available observations, data-supported scope, evidence-family membership, bridge closure, supersession status, chain-segment closure, disconfirmation, and target-specific confidence. It may not silently change the chain kernel, question-compilation grammar, domain cartridge, or settled claim layer.

## Operating sequence

`source -> identity resolution -> data extraction -> design and target classification -> narrative stripping -> bridge audit -> relation-specific validation -> support record -> evidence binder`

Observational evidence begins descriptive or more restricted. Intervention evidence may earn causality for the exact manipulated relation when the comparator, effect distribution, heterogeneity, durability, missingness, measurement, replication, and disconfirmation burden closes. Neither evidence class receives causal status by name.

## Recency

Recent evidence receives retrieval and supersession priority. Recency does not provide causal weight. A newer source supersedes an older relation only when it tests the same target under compatible conditions with stronger, more direct, or disconfirming evidence.

## Output

The ingest runtime emits audited relation-level support records. Canonical integration remains serialized behind explicit adoption.

    required_fields:

      - exact_outcome_definition
      - baseline_distribution
      - comparator_or_counterfactual

      - effect_distribution
      - effect_magnitude

      - missingness_attrition_and_crossover

      - adverse_or_opposing_outcomes
      - measurement_validity_and_noise
      - preregistration_and_outcome_switching
      - analysis_multiplicity
      - replication_and_condition_stability
    rule: >

      resolution: data_controls
      output: stripped_assertion

      - construct_relation
      - measurement_relation
      - target_relation
      - chain_exposure
      - presumptive_class
      - presumed_closure_state
      - eligible_causal_target
    rule: >
      Evidence category and causal target are orthogonal. An intervention may expose one causal relation while
      remaining silent about etiology or endogenous mechanism. An observational study may provide strong descriptive
      evidence while remaining non-causal.

  inference_audit:
    phase: pre_admission
    required_for: every_source
    required_outputs:
      - admitted_observations
      - stripped_assertions
      - bridge_ledger
      - shared_assumption_families
      - non_probative_claims

    rule: >

    blocks_when_unescaped:

      - unitary_defect_claim
      - construct_validity

  design_admission:

      rule: >
        Admit measured distributions and relations at their observed scope. Causal promotion requires affirmative
        closure of temporal order, target specificity, alternative routes, practical magnitude, condition stability,
        negative controls, independent replication, and every required mechanism or scale bridge.
      failed_or_missing_burden: descriptive_or_more_restricted

        - measurement_validity

        - attrition_missingness_and_crossover_bounded
        - replication_or_internal_strength_proportionate_to_claim_scope
        - survival_of_disconfirmation
      pass_rule: >
        A passing intervention can receive causal support for the exact manipulated relation under the tested
        conditions. Very large, consistent, specific, durable effects may be strongly load-bearing.
      scope_rule: >
        Intervention-to-outcome causality does not establish etiology, endogenous mechanism, construct validity,
        or cross-scale explanation without separate closed bridges.
    synthesis:
      starting_role: provenance_only
      rule: >
        Inherit the design, construct, measurement, selection, missing-data, and bridge states of every input.
        Pooling can refine an admissible estimate but cannot repair invalid or incommensurate inputs.

  measurement_admission:
    direct_measurement:
      rule: Directness removes proxy limitation only for the exact variable and scale measured.
    proxy_or_surrogate:
      starting_role: proxy_only
      rule: >
        Preserve the measured proxy relation. Mechanism or causal binding requires a closed physical translation
        path or a distinct direct-dependency record.
    composite_scale_or_self_report:
      rule: >
        Treat the score as the measured variable. Claims about latent state, disorder severity, recovery, or biological
        change require independent measurement and translation.
    named_process_claim:
      rule: >
        A claim that an intervention induces plasticity, normalization, regulation, inflammation reduction, or another
        named process must specify the substrate, variable, direction, timing, persistence, comparator, and dependency
        connecting that change to the target outcome.
      failure_cap: proxy_or_source_scale_only

  causal_admission:
    phase: validation
    trigger: any_source_claiming_cause_control_mechanism_effectiveness_defect_dysfunction_or_biological_change
    rule: >
      Causal weight attaches only to the exact target relation whose design, data, counterfactual, dependency, effect
      distribution, heterogeneity, magnitude, translation, independence, bridges, and disconfirmation checks close.
    hard_failure_conditions:
      - narrative_exceeds_data
      - invalid_or_missing_counterfactual
      - construct_indexed_without_escape
      - proxy_offered_as_untranslated_mechanism
      - pre_post_change_without_valid_comparator
      - intervention_effect_generalized_beyond_manipulated_relation
      - heterogeneous_unbounded_results
      - practically_trivial_or_broadly_overlapping_effect
      - poor_prediction_when_prediction_is_required
      - failed_cross_scale_translation
      - unsupported_inferential_bridge
    hard_failure_output: causal_rejected_for_the_named_relation

  validation_gates:
    practical_significance:
      rule: >
        Statistical separability does not establish effectiveness or functional importance. Evaluate magnitude,
        response distribution, comparator, overlap, noise, threshold, durability, and adverse outcomes.
      failure_cap: descriptive_only
    heterogeneity_control:
      rule: >
        Heterogeneity supports a general claim only when explicit conditions or generated routes account for it.
        Unstable directions and broad overlap defeat unitary or individual-level claims.
      failure_cap: relation_specific_causal_rejection
    comparator_integrity:
      rule: >
        Pre-post change, wait-list comparison, treatment-as-usual, sham, active control, and natural history answer
        different counterfactuals. Bind only the contrast the comparator supports.
      failure_cap: descriptive_only
    durability:
      rule: The time horizon of the claim cannot exceed the observed persistence of the effect.
      failure_cap: short_horizon_only
    proxy_translation:
      rule: Shared terminology and correlated change do not close proxy-to-mechanism translation.
      failure_cap: proxy_only
    scale_translation:
      rule: Evidence crosses scales only through an explicit carried variable and intermediate dependency path.
      failure_cap: source_scale_only
    temporality:
      rule: Concurrent retrospective or postdictive separation cannot supply generative direction by itself.
      postdictive_caps:
        - no_prediction_weight
        - no_direct_generative_grounding
    prediction:
      rule: >
        When a proposed causal relation should predict outcomes, it must outperform realistic baselines and survive
        independent operationalizations and condition changes.
      failure_cap: causal_rejected_for_predictive_claim
    independence:
      rule: >
        Shared cohorts pipelines interventions comparators instruments tasks models or bridges do not count as
        independent closure.
      required_outputs:
        - independence_status
        - evidence_family_id
        - shared_assumption_family_id

  evidence_role_classification:
    roles:

      route_closing: Directly closes a required dependency segment or join under stated conditions.
      route_discriminating: Produces observations that separate surviving routes.
      segment_supporting: Supports one bounded relation without closing the route.
      descriptive_only: Supports only a measured distribution or relation.
      non_probative: Cannot bear on the named conclusion after applicable gates.
    required_outputs:
      - evidence_role_class
      - role_target
      - non_probative_for
      - evidence_family_keys

  status: candidate
  layer: shared_kernel
  purpose: >
    Permit active use of external resources while preventing retrieved text, provider memory, and source authority

  retrieval_permission:
    rule: >
      Use every retrieval capability available to the provider when additional information is needed, including web
      search, scholarly indexes, connected repositories, uploaded files, local corpora, datasets, and user-supplied
      sources. Lack of an ingest packet does not prohibit retrieval for ordinary question answering.
    user_source_boundary: >
      Explicit user limits on browsing, source type, corpus, date range, supplied materials, or connected resources
      constrain retrieval permission. Do not cross a user-imposed source boundary unless the user explicitly relaxes it.
      When the allowed corpus cannot resolve the claim, state the limit and keep the claim unresolved.
    retrieve_when:
      - current or recently changed information could affect the answer
      - the question requires empirical facts effect estimates source-specific claims or corpus state
      - target identity depends on how sources select measure threshold reconstruct or aggregate the target
      - an open counterfactual bridge route physical-chain segment or disconfirmation condition may be resolved
      - a broad claim can be tested against exact quantitative observations
    unavailable_behavior:
      - state that retrieval was unavailable
      - distinguish provider memory from retrieved evidence
      - do not represent remembered corpus claims as verified

  freshness_policy:
    default_discovery_order:
      - search the most recent 12 months first
      - expand to the preceding 24 months when needed
      - retrieve older primary sources required to identify the original measurement method dataset claim or route
      - walk forward to later replications nulls disconfirmation methodological corrections and changed classifications
    rule: >
      Apply a strong recency bias to discovery of the current empirical state. Recency does not increase evidentiary
      or causal weight by itself. Older direct measurements may remain more probative than recent reviews summaries
      commentary or consensus statements.
    current_state_requirement: >
      When the question asks what is currently known, retrieve enough recent material to test whether the older
      account remains current rather than repeating the provider's training-era summary.

      empirical closure when the underlying records are available. A claim about a navigation source itself may use

      Prefer independent evidence families and distinct operationalizations. Multiple sources that repeat the same
      cohort instrument pipeline threshold database or inferential bridge remain one assumption family.

  universal_admission_rule:
    rule: >
      Every assertion obtained through retrieval must pass the same target-identity construct exact-relation design

    source_authority_does_not_upgrade:
      - publication venue
      - peer review
      - author reputation
      - citation volume
      - review or meta-analysis status
      - guideline or consensus status
      - institutional source
      - search ranking
      - recency alone

    forbidden_uses:
      - close target identity
      - establish corpus state
      - supply an effect magnitude distribution overlap or predictive result
      - close a causal bridge physical segment metabolic relation mechanism or scale translation
      - rescue a rejected premise
    rule: >
      Provider memory is a lead generator, not admitted evidence. Claims drawn from memory remain unverified until
      bound to a retrieved or user-supplied record and processed through the model.

      - effect magnitude

      - nominal and independent sample units

      - missingness attrition crossover and analysis exclusions

statistical_qualifiers:
  status: candidate
  layer: shared_kernel
  purpose: >
    Convert reported numerical results into claim-specific qualifiers for magnitude, precision, separation,
    dependence, selection optimism, stability, calibration, multiplicity, and transport. These qualifiers constrain
    promotion of the exact relation. They do not create one aggregate study-quality score.

  governing_rules:
    - A numerical result is qualified for the claim actually made, not for a paper or model as a whole.
    - Strength on one qualifier cannot repair failure on another qualifier.
    - Statistical significance cannot supply practical magnitude, individual separation, calibration, stability, or transport.
    - Missing inputs remain unresolved. Do not replace unavailable distributions or pipeline details with favorable assumptions.
    - Use empirical distributions and independent evaluation data before parametric approximations when both are available.
    - No universal sample-size, accuracy, overlap, AUC, calibration, or feature-count cutoff applies across claims.

  claim_record:
    required:
      - exact_numerical_claim
      - target_relation
      - measurement_unit
      - independent_sampling_unit
      - population_and_sampling_frame
      - estimator_or_model
      - evaluation_design
      - practical_threshold_when_claimed
    rule: >
      Remove the headline metric from its narrative wrapper and reconstruct the operation that generated it. Bind every
      qualifier to the exact outcome, population, threshold, pipeline, and evaluation design.

  information_and_dependence:
    required_when_available:
      - nominal_row_count
      - independent_unit_count
      - positive_and_negative_evaluation_counts
      - cluster_count_and_cluster_size
      - repeated_measurement_structure
      - family_site_operator_or_time_dependencies
      - sampling_or_analysis_weights
      - intraclass_correlation
    calculations_when_inputs_permit:
      cluster_design_effect: "1 + (mean_cluster_size - 1) * intraclass_correlation"
      cluster_effective_n: "nominal_n / cluster_design_effect"
      weighted_effective_n: "sum(weights)^2 / sum(weights^2)"
    rules:
      - Rows measurements trials images windows and augmented copies do not become independent population units by count.
      - The effective information is bounded by the highest dependency unit relevant to the claim.
      - When dependence is known but cannot be quantified, effective sample size remains unresolved rather than nominal.

  magnitude_and_precision:
    required_when_reported_or_derivable:
      - point_estimate
      - uncertainty_interval
      - interval_method
      - standard_error_or_sampling_distribution
      - practical_effect_threshold
      - direction_and_units
    derived_when_inputs_permit:
      relative_uncertainty: "confidence_interval_half_width / abs(point_estimate)"
      threshold_resolution: "confidence_interval_width / practical_effect_threshold"
    status_rules:
      - If the interval spans materially opposing directions, direction is unresolved.
      - If the interval spans trivial and practically consequential effects, practical magnitude is unresolved.
      - A narrow interval centered near zero establishes a small effect rather than importance.
      - A point estimate without adequate uncertainty cannot establish the magnitude claimed.

  distributional_separation:
    required_when_the_claim_entails_individual_or_category_separation:
      - group_or_class_distributions
      - variance_or_quantiles
      - empirical_overlap_when_available
      - standardized_effect_when_available
      - out_of_sample_discrimination
    approximation_when_compatible:
      assumptions:
        - approximately_normal_distributions
        - equal_variance
        - standardized_mean_difference
      overlap_coefficient: "2 * Phi(-abs(d) / 2)"
      idealized_balanced_accuracy: "Phi(abs(d) / 2)"
    rules:
      - State every assumption used for a distributional approximation.
      - Near-total overlap is positive evidence against individual defect category separator or unitary-state promotion.
      - A group-average displacement may remain valid even when individual assignment fails.
      - Multivariate discrimination exceeding univariate separation requires an identified added information route and leakage audit.

  classification_information:
    raw_accuracy_default_status: unresolved_without_context
    required:
      - independent_test_positive_count
      - independent_test_negative_count
      - confusion_matrix
      - class_prevalence_in_evaluation_sample
      - intended_deployment_prevalence
      - no_information_or_simple_baseline
      - sensitivity_and_specificity
      - balanced_accuracy
      - predictive_values_at_relevant_prevalence
      - threshold_selection_rule
      - uncertainty_for_each_claimed_metric
    derive_when_inputs_permit:
      - majority_class_accuracy
      - balanced_accuracy
      - sensitivity
      - specificity
      - positive_and_negative_predictive_value
      - likelihood_ratios
      - Matthews_correlation_coefficient
      - expected_false_positive_and_false_negative_counts_at_deployment_prevalence
    rules:
      - Accuracy alone receives no support weight.
      - Artificially balanced evaluation prevalence cannot be transported into deployment predictive values.
      - Cross-validation predictions from overlapping training sets do not become independent test observations.
      - When evaluation class counts cannot estimate the claimed performance with useful precision, performance magnitude is unresolved.

  pipeline_isolation_and_selection:
    data_dependent_operations:
      - imputation
      - normalization_or_scaling
      - harmonization
      - outlier_rules
      - confound_adjustment
      - feature_construction
      - feature_selection
      - dimensionality_reduction
      - model_family_selection
      - hyperparameter_tuning
      - threshold_selection
      - calibration
    rules:
      - Every data-dependent operation must be fitted inside the training portion of each evaluation split.
      - Any evaluation data used to choose preprocessing features models hyperparameters thresholds or reporting makes that evaluation part of model construction.
      - Nested evaluation can estimate selection-corrected performance within the sampled population. It does not establish transport.
      - An unreported or unisolated adaptive search leaves selection optimism unresolved.
      - Similar aggregate performance across viable pipelines does not establish stable individual assignments.

  optimism_ledger:
    required_when_available:
      - apparent_training_or_reused_evaluation_performance
      - internally_corrected_performance
      - locked_external_performance
      - prospective_operational_performance
    calculations:
      selection_optimism: "apparent_performance - internally_corrected_performance"
      transport_loss: "internally_corrected_performance - locked_external_performance"
      implementation_loss: "locked_external_performance - prospective_operational_performance"
    rule: >
      A large drop at any stage is positive evidence that the earlier estimate depended on selection, sampling,
      environment, or implementation. It is not merely a lower-weight version of the original claim.

  resampling_stability:
    full_pipeline_requirement: >
      Repeat the entire data-dependent pipeline across bootstrap samples or repeated development samples. Reusing a fixed
      selected feature set or tuned pipeline measures conditional variance only and cannot establish construction stability.
    extract_when_available:
      - individual_prediction_distribution
      - classification_flip_rate
      - coefficient_sign_and_magnitude_stability
      - feature_selection_frequency
      - rank_stability
      - threshold_stability
      - calibration_parameter_distribution
      - performance_distribution
    rules:
      - Material prediction or classification changes under minor resampling are positive evidence against a stable individual separator.
      - Stable average performance cannot rescue unstable individual assignments.
      - Feature recurrence without prediction stability does not establish a stable route.

  learning_curve_and_assurance:
    required_when_generalized_or_deployment_performance_is_claimed:
      - performance_across_increasing_training_sizes
      - uncertainty_across_increasing_training_sizes
      - calibration_across_increasing_training_sizes
      - prediction_instability_across_increasing_training_sizes
      - prespecified_stopping_or_acceptance_criteria
    preferred_target: "probability_that_performance_lies_in_an_acceptable_range"
    rules:
      - A high small-sample estimate that declines with increasing sample size is optimism evidence.
      - A materially changing learning curve means performance has not converged.
      - Acceptable expected performance cannot replace an adequate probability of acceptable realized performance.

  calibration_and_proper_scoring:
    required_when_probabilities_or_risks_are_claimed:
      - calibration_intercept
      - calibration_slope
      - calibration_curve
      - Brier_score_or_other_proper_score
      - log_loss_or_other_strictly_proper_score_when_available
      - threshold_specific_decision_utility_when_action_is_claimed
    rules:
      - Discrimination ranking calibration and decision utility are separate relations.
      - A calibration summary such as expected calibration error cannot establish probability quality by itself.
      - Recalibration on the evaluation set makes that set part of model construction unless a later untouched test remains.

  multiplicity_and_researcher_selection:
    required_when_available:
      - number_of_outcomes
      - number_of_tests
      - number_of_subgroups
      - number_of_time_windows
      - number_of_models_and_hyperparameter_settings
      - correction_or_hierarchical_model
      - preregistration_and_outcome_switching
      - multiverse_or_specification_results
    simple_check_when_inputs_permit:
      expected_null_positives: "number_of_tests * alpha"
    rules:
      - A selected positive result from an incompletely reported search space has unresolved selection burden.
      - Dependent tests and adaptive search prevent naive test-count arithmetic from proving adequate correction.
      - When specification results exist report sign stability magnitude range and the fraction clearing practical thresholds.

  transport_distribution:
    required_when_scope_exceeds_one_sample_or_setting:
      - performance_by_site_cohort_time_operator_device_or_population
      - between_setting_variance
      - worst_setting_performance
      - transport_loss
      - prediction_interval_for_a_new_setting_when_estimable
      - prospective_operational_validation_when_use_is_claimed
    rules:
      - One external validation establishes performance in one additional setting rather than universal validation.
      - Generalization requires a distribution of performance across relevant settings or an explicitly bounded target setting.
      - Pooled performance cannot hide setting-level reversal collapse or severe calibration drift.

  output_record:
    required:
      - target_relation
      - information_status
      - magnitude_status
      - precision_status
      - separation_status
      - classification_status
      - pipeline_isolation_status
      - optimism_status
      - stability_status
      - calibration_status
      - multiplicity_status
      - transport_status
      - admitted_numerical_relation
      - blocked_promotions
      - unresolved_inputs
    rule: >
      Return the strongest numerical relation that survives every qualifier required by the claim. Preserve exact
      observations while blocking individual category causal mechanistic clinical or deployment promotion when the
      corresponding qualifier fails.

  forbidden_patterns:
    - one_global_statistical_quality_score
    - raw_accuracy_as_validation
    - nominal_rows_as_independent_sample_size
    - statistical_significance_as_practical_importance
    - group_mean_difference_as_individual_separator
    - internal_cross_validation_as_external_transport
    - nested_cross_validation_as_deployment_validation
    - aggregate_performance_as_individual_assignment_stability
    - one_external_validation_as_universal_validation
    - calibration_metric_without_proper_score_or_untouched_evaluation
    - selected_best_pipeline_without_search_burden
    - missing_uncertainty_replaced_by_favorable_assumption
